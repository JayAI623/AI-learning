KV Cache: QKV + Mask View

Understand KV cache by tracking Q/K/V projections and attention score/mask computation step-by-step.

1

Q/K/V Computation Per Decode Step

At decode step t: without cache, recompute Q,K,V for all 1..t tokens. With cache, compute only q_t,k_t,v_t and reuse historical K,V.

🚀

Compute savings come from removing repeated K/V projections on history.

2

Attention Scores + Causal Mask

Without cache, naive recomputation forms full t×t scores and applies mask to all rows. With cache, decoding needs only the last row: q_t · K_{1:t}^T + mask_t.

⚡

Generation uses the current token output, so only the last attention row is required.

3

What KV Cache Stores

Cache keeps historical K and V only. Each new token appends one K-row and one V-row.

📦

Per layer, per head: K,V with shape [batch, heads, seq_len, head_dim].

4

Why Not Cache Q?

Past Q vectors are not queried again in future decoding steps. Future steps repeatedly read past K,V, not past Q.

💡

Cache what has future reuse: K/V yes, Q no.
